5
Provider Responsibilities
Sunnyday Carers commits to:
  • Deliver agreed supports safely, with skill, and in a timely manner
  • Treat the participant with dignity and respect at all times
  • Consult the participant on decisions that affect them, including who provides their supports and when
  • Give the participant information about all service options and assist them to make informed choices
  • Maintain the privacy and confidentiality of the participant's personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
  • Employ support workers with valid NDIS Worker Screening clearances
  • Maintain appropriate insurances including public liability and professional indemnity
  • Follow Sunnyday Carers' incident management, complaints, and feedback procedures
  • Report any notifiable incidents to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission within required timeframes
  • Provide at least 7 days' written notice if unable to continue delivering agreed supports
6
Participant Rights & Responsibilities
Your rights under this agreement
  • Make decisions about the supports you receive and how they are delivered
  • Choose who provides your supports and change providers with reasonable notice
  • Have your privacy, dignity, and cultural identity respected
  • Make a complaint at any time without fear of negative consequences
  • Receive a copy of this agreement and any changes to it
  • Contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission at any time — 1800 035 544
Your responsibilities
  • Notify Sunnyday Carers if you cannot attend a scheduled support session (48 hours notice preferred)
  • Inform Sunnyday Carers of relevant changes to your NDIS plan, contact details, or living arrangements
  • Treat Sunnyday Carers staff with courtesy and respect
  • Provide a safe and respectful environment for support workers visiting your home
7
Cancellations & Short-Notice Policy
Consistent with the NDIS Pricing Arrangements, Sunnyday Carers may charge for a cancelled support if the participant or their representative provides less than 2 clear business days' notice (short-notice cancellation). The cancellation rate is 100% of the agreed fee for the support session.
Cancellations made with more than 2 clear business days' notice will not incur a fee. Sunnyday Carers will not charge for cancellations caused by the provider.
8
Ending or Changing this Agreement
Either party may end this agreement by providing 14 days written notice. If the participant's safety is at risk, Sunnyday Carers may suspend services with immediate effect and will work with the participant, their representative, and the NDIS to arrange alternative supports.
Any changes to this agreement must be agreed to in writing by both parties. A review of this agreement will occur at minimum every 12 months or when the participant's NDIS plan changes.
9
Complaints & Feedback
Sunnyday Carers welcomes feedback and is committed to resolving complaints promptly and fairly. You may raise a complaint by:
  • Speaking directly to your support worker or house manager
  • Contacting our Participant Experience team: 1300 786 692
  • Emailing feedback@sunnydaycarers.com.au
  • Using the NDIS Commission's online complaints form at ndiscommission.gov.au
  • Calling the NDIS Commission directly: 1800 035 544
10
Privacy & Information Sharing
Sunnyday Carers will collect, use, and store personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. Information about the participant will only be shared with:
  • NDIS support workers and coordinators involved in delivering supports
  • The NDIS (NDIA) as required for plan management and claiming
  • Health practitioners, emergency services, or legal authorities where required by law or to prevent harm
  • Other service providers nominated by the participant
The participant's personal information will not be used for marketing purposes or shared with third parties without consent.
